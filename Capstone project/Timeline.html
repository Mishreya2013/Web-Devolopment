Japanese Timeline


Summary

Japan's timeline begins with the prehistoric Jomon period (c. 10,000–300 BCE), followed by the introduction of wet rice cultivation in the Yayoi period (c. 300 BCE–300 CE). The Kofun and Asuka periods saw the unification of Japan and the introduction of Buddhism. The classic Nara (710–794 CE) and Heian (794–1185 CE) periods established the imperial court. A feudal system emerged with the establishment of the first shogunate during the Kamakura period (1185–1333 CE). The Edo period (1603–1868 CE) was characterized by isolation and a capital at Edo (Tokyo). Meiji (1868–1912), Taisho (1912–1926), and Showa (1926–1989) periods marked rapid industrialization, expansion, and eventual devastation from World War II. The Heisei (1989–2019) and Reiwa periods have seen the rise of technological development and recovery from major natural disasters.


Key Periods and Events:

Prehistoric/Ancient Japan (up to 710 CE):

  • Jomon Period (c. 10,000–300 BCE): Prehistoric hunter-gatherer culture.

  • Yayoi Period (c. 300 BCE–300 CE): Introduction of rice cultivation and pottery wheels.

  • Kofun Period (c. 300–645 CE): Emergence of powerful clan rulers under the Yamato Court and large burial mounds (kofun).

  • Asuka Period (645–710 CE): Great reforms (Taika Reforms) and the introduction of Buddhism from China.

Classical Japan (710–1185 CE):

  • Nara Period (710–794 CE): First permanent capital established at Nara, modeled after the Chinese capital.
    • Heian Period (794–1185 CE): Imperial court moved to Heian-kyō (Kyoto).
