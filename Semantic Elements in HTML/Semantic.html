Dark Matter

Beside dark energy, there is another source of gravity in space. Recently, scientists have observed the pull of this interesting source of gravity towards other celestial objects, for instance, stars.
Yet, this is not the matter we recognise, but it is also not a black hole. Scientists have termed this as dark matter, for now. Fun fact, 27% of the universe is made up of dark matter.
Dark matter, originally known as missing mass, was found by Swiss-American astronomer Fritz Zwicky in 1933.
